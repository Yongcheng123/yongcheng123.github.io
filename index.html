About Me

I am a dedicated and analytical Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Old Dominion University with a strong background in machine learning, deep learning, and data analysis. My research focuses on developing innovative computational tools, particularly in the realm of bioinformatics and dietary analysis using deep learning techniques. I am passionate about solving complex problems and collaborating on impactful research projects.

My work involves applying state-of-the-art deep learning approaches to solve challenging problems in structural biology, specifically in the segmentation and analysis of protein structures from cryo-electron microscopy data. I have published multiple papers in top-tier conferences and journals, and I am committed to advancing the field through innovative research and collaboration.

Research

My research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, computer vision, and computational biology. I focus on developing novel deep learning methodologies for analyzing biological structures from cryo-electron microscopy (cryo-EM) density maps.

Specifically, I work on protein secondary structure segmentation, developing advanced loss functions and neural network architectures to improve the accuracy of automated structure detection. My work contributes to the structural biology community by providing tools that accelerate the process of protein structure determination.
